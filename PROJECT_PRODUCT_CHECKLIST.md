# ProjectXBidX Product Checklist

This checklist captures product ideas from the April 2026 brainstorming session so they do not get lost while the core platform continues to move forward.

## Near-Term Focus

- [ ] Make the customer AI project flow feel clear, guided, and trustworthy.
- [ ] Reframe "AI Scope Check" as a customer-friendly scope-building step before contractors bid.
- [ ] Make it obvious that the customer is creating a reference scope and planning estimate, not accepting a final contractor price.
- [ ] Let customers clearly confirm, skip, restore, and eventually add their own scope items before posting.
- [ ] Show customers how confirmed scope items become the checklist contractors use for real-world bids.

## Product Positioning

- [ ] Describe ProjectXBidX as a construction estimating and bidding platform, not just a website.
- [ ] Keep the core marketplace aligned with `project-vision.md`: customers post projects, qualified contractors submit sealed bids, and the platform protects trust through clear scope, credentials, messaging, and history.
- [ ] Keep "web app" or "platform" as the preferred product language for the current browser-based product.

## Customer Scope Builder

- [ ] Treat the AI flow as the first major customer experience: the customer describes the project, AI identifies the project type, AI proposes standard requirements, and the customer confirms what applies.
- [ ] Add a clearer step-by-step explanation before the AI runs.
- [ ] Add clearer trust language around what the AI can and cannot do.
- [ ] Add better next-step language after the AI returns proposed scope items.
- [ ] Consider adding customer-entered custom scope items to the pre-post checklist.
- [ ] Consider restoring the clarification-question flow once the simpler scope checklist feels right.

## Professional Estimator Marketplace

- [ ] Explore a future estimator role separate from customer and contractor accounts.
- [ ] Let professional estimators publish material takeoffs, estimate packages, or bid-ready scope packages.
- [ ] Tie each estimate package directly to the estimator's name, reputation, reviews, and trust history.
- [ ] Let contractors buy or access trusted takeoffs so each project does not need to be estimated from scratch by every bidder.
- [ ] Consider future integrations with builders exchanges so public plan-room projects can show whether trusted takeoffs already exist.
- [ ] Preserve the principle that estimate quality matters because a bad short estimate can bankrupt a contractor.

## Standard Automated Bid Verticals

- [ ] Explore highly repeatable project types where bids can be mostly automated.
- [ ] Start with lawn mowing / lawn care as a possible standard-bid vertical.
- [ ] Investigate map-based area measurement inputs, such as property polygon area in square feet.
- [ ] Let contractors define standard pricing rules for repeatable services.
- [ ] Use AI to explain and assemble the quote, but keep measured quantities and contractor-approved rates as the pricing source of truth.

## Future Questions

- [ ] Should "paid estimate pools" remain contractor-bid incentives while "estimator marketplace" becomes a separate feature?
- [ ] Should estimators be a third role, or should trusted estimators be a specialized contractor profile type?
- [ ] How should estimator liability, disputes, refunds, and quality ratings work?
- [ ] Which project categories are safe enough for automated quote generation first?
